What did you do last week?

Last week, I finally conceptualized what is happening during back propagation. I found a video on youtube (by 3Blue1Brown or something like that) that explained the concept of NN's using visuals that really helped. One of the big hold ups that I had with this understanding was that when I was building my NN, I was feeding the data in aggregate rather than looking at what happens with a single input observation. Looking at it from a single observation strips away a lot of the complexity because now you cut away an extra step in understanding of how the backpropagation process is averaged across the input observations. When you look at the aggregate that way, you can't conceptually isolate things like "for this single observation with a single classification, the activations look like this. And when the activations look like this, we can compare that to what we want the output activations to look like to understand why the back propagation is changing weights the way that it is." Looking at it from that perpective helped me understand what is actually happening during back prop.
